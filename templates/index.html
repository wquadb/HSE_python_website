Brief data overview






Relative Strength Index (RSI) parameter overview

$$ RSI = 100 - \frac{100}{1 + RS} $$

$$ \begin{align*} RS & = \frac{\text{AG}}{\text{AL}} \end{align*} $$

$$ \begin{align*} \text{AG (Average Gain)} & = \frac{\text{Sum of gains over } n \text{ periods}}{n} \end{align*} $$

$$ \begin{align*} \text{AL (Average Loss)} & = \frac{\text{Sum of losses over } n \text{ periods}}{n} \end{align*} $$


Which is important, RSI is firstly used to determine price reversal on the timeframe from 1 week to several months. Let's look at commonly used periods for calculating RSI.


Notice that the more period of RSI the less noise on more accurate trend representation we have
